## Executive Summary

McKeown Kraai Professional CPAs (MKP) is a well-established 40-year Southwest Michigan firm with strong local search visibility and a genuinely positive community reputation — but a significant digital divide is holding them back. The site earns #1 Google rankings for four of five local branded searches while simultaneously delivering one of the worst mobile experiences we measure: a 7.9-second load time on phones loses the majority of visitors before a single word loads. The WHO WE SERVE section signals real niche depth in construction, agriculture, manufacturing, and startups, but the copy doesn't deliver on that promise — services are described generically when specific industry language would speak directly to ideal clients. The biggest opportunity is converting MKP's substantial offline credibility — a 2025 MICPA Small Firm Award, Big 4 partner backgrounds, and 40 years in the community — into the kind of online authority that compounds into inbound leads.

Your site scored 58/100 on mobile performance — landing squarely in the failing range. The most alarming finding is a 7.9-second Largest Contentful Paint on mobile: that is the time it takes for the main content to appear on a phone screen. Studies consistently show that visitors abandon pages after 3 seconds, meaning the majority of mobile visitors never see your content at all. Compounding this, the site fails three critical mobile usability checks: the viewport is not correctly configured, tap targets (buttons and links) are too small for reliable finger taps, and font sizes are flagged as too small for comfortable reading. The one bright spot is perfect layout stability (CLS of 0) — the page doesn't jump around as it loads. Mobile now accounts for the majority of local business searches; this is the highest-priority issue in this entire report.

MKP's niche communication sits at a frustrating middle ground: enough signals to attract attention, not enough depth to convert. The firm names four clear niches — construction, agriculture, manufacturing, and startups — but the supporting copy doesn't deliver the industry-specific language and pain points that make ideal clients feel spoken to. Outcome framing is the relative strength here: phrases like 'help you grow your business' and 'take the stress out of running your business' speak to results rather than just describing firm capabilities. Where the site falls short is social proof: there are no testimonials tied to specific industries, no case studies with outcomes, and no certifications or credentials that signal niche depth. The niche CTAs are entirely absent — a construction contractor visiting the construction page encounters the same generic 'Go' button as every other visitor.

### Detected Niches

- **Real estate & construction** (confirmed) — Homepage explicitly lists 'Construction' in WHO WE SERVE section with dedicated copy: 'Accounting solutions designed for the Construction Industry.'
- **Agriculture** (confirmed) — Homepage lists 'Agriculture' in WHO WE SERVE section: 'We'll dig into the numbers and offer deeper insight to help you grow your business.'
- **Manufacturing** (confirmed) — Homepage lists 'Manufacturing' in WHO WE SERVE with copy: 'Get dedicated, strategic insight into your accounting for a healthier financial future.'
- **Business startups** (confirmed) — Homepage lists 'Business Startups' as primary audience: 'When it's hard to get started...we help get your new business moving.'
- **Restaurants & hospitality** (weak signal) — Blog content references tip tax legislation; restaurant imagery in articles but no dedicated services page or direct messaging.

### Invisible but High-Opportunity Niches

- **Healthcare & medical practices** — Medical practices need complex tax planning and financial management; Michigan's healthcare market is substantial and MKP's advisory capability is a natural fit.
- **Trucking & logistics** — Trucking firms need IFTA reporting, per diem deductions, and owner-operator tax planning — all areas where a full-service CPA firm has a clear advantage over general preparers.
- **Home services** — Plumbers, electricians, HVAC, and landscapers in Southwest Michigan share accounting complexity with construction clients MKP already serves.
- **High-net-worth individuals** — Partner credentials and advisory capabilities suggest estate and wealth planning capacity that is completely absent from the site.
- **Nonprofits** — Form 990 compliance and grant accounting are specialized; Michigan nonprofit market represents meaningful volume for a full-service firm.

### Top 3 Highest-Impact Improvements

1. Create niche-specific service pages for Construction, Agriculture, and Manufacturing that go beyond a tagline — each should include relevant tax strategies (cost segregation, farm income averaging, Section 179), specific pain points, and at least one outcome-anchored testimonial or case study.
2. Replace the three current CTAs ('APPLY HERE,' 'Go,' 'MKP Culture') with prospect-facing calls to action tied to the visitor's likely intent, such as 'Schedule a Free Consultation,' 'See How We Help Contractors,' or 'Get Your Tax Planning Started.'
3. Add niche-specific proof to each industry section: a specific outcome number ('Helped a local contractor recover $87K through cost segregation'), a client industry and challenge, or a credential/certification relevant to that industry.
